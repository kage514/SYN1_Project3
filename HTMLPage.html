About the Project

Weed Warrior is a public-interest activity where volunteers work together to remove invasive weeds and reduce their impact on native plants.

What we did

We identified common invasive weeds, removed them safely, and collected all plant waste for proper disposal.

Why it matters

Invasive weeds can outcompete native plants, reduce biodiversity, and damage local ecosystems. Removing them is a simple action with lasting benefits.

How we stayed safe

We wore gloves, avoided unknown plants, used tools carefully, and washed hands afterward. (Always follow local guidelines and supervision.)

Our Motivation

Problem we noticed

We observed that invasive weeds were spreading in our local park, crowding out native plants and making trails less welcoming for families and wildlife.

What we wanted to achieve

We wanted to take practical action: remove weeds, raise awareness, and encourage others to care for shared public spaces.

What we learned

Small teamwork can create visible change. We also learned how to distinguish invasive species, and why consistent community efforts matter.
