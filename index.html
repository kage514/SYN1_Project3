Why this activity matters and what we hope to change.

The Problem of Invasive Weeds

Invasive weeds can spread rapidly. They occupy the space of local plants and, thus, destroying biodiversity. Moreover, invasive weeds like brome grass or ice plant make fires more likely to occur (from Theodore Payne Foundation ).

Why Early Removal Matters

Invasive plants often go through a stage called the lag phase, in which the plants are present in the ecosystem but spreading slowly. Because the invasion has not yet reached its rapid expansion stage, it is much easier to control or remove the species. For this reason, early actions like Weed Warrior is one of the most effective strategies for protecting native ecosystems.

Community Action Matters

Protecting the ecosystems is not only the responsibility of scientists or policymakers, but also of us as local communities. Volunteer activities definitely play an essential role here. We advocate participating in environmental protecting activities, by which community members can directly contribute to protecting biodiversity and maintaining the health of local ecosystems. Small collective actions can make a meaningful difference for environmental protection.

Team Reflections

What did we get from picking weeds.

D

Dante

Going out to nature and picking up Weeds felt so satisfying. I felt so connected to nature, being exposed to the wild while the sun was rising was such a unique experience. I felt I had a positive contribution to nature by getting rid of invasive species. This activity made me realize how much positive impact I and many others can have on the environment in our community with so little effort. Picking up weeds is an activity that even kids can do, which can help save our local native species.

S

Sarthak

Going to the scripps local reserve with my friends in my SYN 1 section was one of my favorite in-class moments at UCSD. Being able to contribute to the habitat there felt like a privilege and being there in the early morning with the sun rising allowed me to feel one with nature. Our lead also taught us a lot about invasive species and the impacts they can have if we don't get rid of them. Picking weeds may be tiring, but it definitely is very rewarding! Although I know that just me contributing to our planet isn't enough to make a change, I really hope that people read my experience with my friends and try to take on their own journey. If we all take the tiny steps we can, I firmly believe we can take on climate change easily and have a beautiful mother earth.

I

Issac

Picking weeds with weed warriors was a really inspiring experience. It was great being able to go out and do some habitat restoration, getting really getting hands on with nature and the environment. Even though we had to wake up early, it was definitely worth it. The most inspiring part for me was listening to the lead talk about how as a result of people coming to pick weeds for a few years, they actually made some great progress restoring this habitat, and it felt pretty special that I could be a part of that too. Hopefully that can also inspire anyone else reading this to go pick weeds with Weed Warriors too!
